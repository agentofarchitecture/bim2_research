# Rejected / Noisy Sources

Last updated: 2026-05-23.

## Rejection rules

- Reject generic AEC AI marketing pages with no named workflow, API, example, repo, benchmark, schema, or validation path.
- Reject BIM/digital twin content that never gets more specific than “single source of truth”.
- Reject SEO listicles about “top AI architecture tools” unless they point to primary sources worth following.
- Treat vendor pages as candidate-only until implementation details, docs, changelogs, or credible users are found.

## Current rejected/noisy patterns

### “AI will design whole buildings” pages

- Reason: Usually high hype, low implementation detail, poor actionability for Rhino Mind.
- What to save instead: concrete agent tasks such as massing option generation, site analysis, model QA, property validation, or drawing/documentation automation.

### Generic “digital twin” marketing

- Reason: Often vague and enterprise-oriented; low value unless tied to IFC, IDS, bSDD, openCDE, sensors, API/data model, or a real handover workflow.
- What to save instead: specific schemas, integrations, and evidence that a practice can maintain the data.

### Unverified people inferred only from product pages

- Reason: The Top 100 should be evidence-led. Do not promote people merely because a company is relevant.
- What to save instead: GitHub contributors, paper authors, standards contributors, public talks, docs authors, or practitioners with specific technical writing.
